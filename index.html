TOC Manager
Guide your audience through Notion content on Super with a clean, linear flow.

Notion + Super → a great CMS… missing a guided flow

Notion is a terrific platform for writing, organizing, and maintaining content. With Super as the front end, you get fast hosting, custom domains, themes, routing, and powerful site controls. It’s a strong foundation for courses, presentations, and documentation.

But there’s a gap: neither Notion nor Super ships a way to define a site-wide learning path with Next and Previous buttons. Those controls are critical to lead students through a class (or attendees through a talk) without getting lost.

This app fills that gap by letting you craft an ordered table of contents (TOC), then inject it into your Super site together with a sticky Prev/Next footer.

What Super adds on top of Notion

  • Fast static front‑end with theming, fonts, and custom CSS/JS
  • Custom domains, SEO controls, page routing, and performance optimizations
  • Site‑wide code injection (Head / Body / CSS) — perfect for our manifest + sticky footer

How the TOC Manager works

1) Load your site’s structure

Provide your Super sitemap (/sitemap.xml). The app parses every URL and builds a list of pages. You can also import a previous manifest.json to restore your last order and titles.

2) Organize modules and lessons

  • Drag module headers to reorder modules (collapsed by default)
  • Drag lesson rows to reorder within/between modules
  • Edit titles or toggle Visible for pages you want to hide from the flow
  • New pages are marked New; removed pages are marked Stale

3) Publish to Super

  1. Copy Head — pastes a <script id="lesson-manifest"> JSON block that defines your linear order.
  2. Copy CSS — optional; styles the sticky footer.
  3. Copy Body — optional; adds the sticky footer HTML and the tiny JS that reads your manifest and wires up Prev/Next.
  4. Publish on Super and hard refresh your site.

Why the sitemap matters

Your Super site automatically exposes /sitemap.xml. We use it to:

  • Discover all published pages
  • Detect new links since your last session
  • Flag stale links that no longer exist

Save your work

Click Export manifest.json to save your current ordering. Next time, Import manifest.json first, then load the sitemap to merge new pages without losing your carefully curated order.

Result: a clean, deterministic learning path with sticky Prev/Next across your entire Super site.

Quick start

  1. Open TOC Manager.
  2. Paste your Super sitemap URL and click Load sitemap.
  3. (Optional) Import a previous manifest.json.
  4. Reorder modules and lessons; edit titles; toggle visibility.
  5. Copy Head (required), and optionally CSS + Body, into Super → Code.
  6. Publish on Super and verify the sticky footer on any page that’s in your flow.

The sticky footer appears only on pages that are in your linear_order.
